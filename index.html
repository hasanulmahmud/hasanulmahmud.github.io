About Me

I have over five years of teaching and mentoring experience at both undergraduate and graduate levels, delivering courses, labs, and research guidance that foster strong technical foundations and problem-solving skills. My instructional work emphasizes hands-on learning, curriculum development, and individualized academic support to help students excel in computer science and engineering. My research focuses on designing energy-efficient, low-latency deep neural networks for edge and cloud environments, with particular emphasis on converting autoencoders for secure and high-performance inference under resource constraints. This work spans model compression, SLO-aware deployment, and adversarial robustness, aiming to make AI systems both more efficient and resilient.
